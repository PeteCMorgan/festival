Welcome to the Festival

The Common Grounds Community Festival at the Lookout is a free, inclusive community event designed to bring people together through shared learning, creativity, environmental stewardship and local culture. Join us for a day of hands-on workshops, guided experiences and creative activities that celebrate sustainable living, local food systems, bush regeneration and the arts.

Open to all residents across the Wollongong Local Government Area, with a special invitation to coastal Wollongong communities. All workshops and activities are designed to be informal, flexible and accessible‚Äîengage at your own pace.

Festival Activities

Permaculture Workshops

Hands-on introduction to syntropic agriculture and regenerative food systems. Learn about layered planting, food forests, soil regeneration, mulching and water-wise practices.

Local Food Swap

A community-led food and seed swap encouraging sharing of surplus produce, strengthening food resilience and reducing waste. Bring what you can, take what you need.

Bush Regeneration Tours

Guided tours exploring sub-tropical and coastal vegetation. Focus on native plant identification, weed management, erosion control and caring for bushland adjoining the Royal National Park.

Creative Workshops

Pottery and art workshops facilitated by local artists, offering inclusive, nature-inspired creative experiences for participants of all ages and skill levels.

Live Local Music

Simple acoustic performances by local musicians. Unamplified or lightly amplified to ensure minimal noise impact, perfect for our beautiful bushland setting.

Community Benefit

  • Strengthens social connection and community bonds
  • Provides accessible environmental education
  • Supports local artists and musicians
  • Encourages sustainable food production and sharing
  • Fosters long-term stewardship of local landscapes
  • Celebrates our connection to Dharawal Country

Location & Setting

The Lookout, Otford

Address: 40 Lady Wakehurst Drive, Otford

Nestled on the edge of the magnificent Royal National Park on Dharawal Country, our venue offers a unique bushland setting that celebrates the natural beauty of coastal Wollongong. The property borders sub-tropical and coastal vegetation communities, providing the perfect backdrop for our festival of sustainability and connection to Country.

Easily accessible for all Wollongong residents, with a special welcome to those from coastal communities. The site offers a peaceful, inspiring environment where learning and creativity flourish.
